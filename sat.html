SAT Overview

The SAT can be broken down into two main sections Evidence-Based Reading and Writing (EBRW) and Math. There are two subsections under EBRW: Reading first, then and Writing and Language. Under Math, there are also two subsections: With Calculator and Without Calculator.

The SAT broken down into sections and questions and minutes per section

The SAT is an extensive exam that can span up to 4 hours. If you are taking the optional essay, you will have three breaks during your sitting. Otherwise, you will have two breaks.

What To Bring on Test Day
  • ID (passports, school ID card, national ID card)/li>
  • Admission ticket
  • Several No.2 pencils
  • Erasers
  • Approved calculator (most calculators work, just no smartwatches, laptops, or calculators that connect to bluetooth, the internet,or make noise)
  • Small snack for breaks (optional)
  • Waterbottle with no labelling (optional)
  • Watch to pace yourself (optional)
